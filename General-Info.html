Meet Entry Procedure

Entries must be submitted to the host club 2 to 4 weeks before the swim meet. Late fees will not be accepted. The schedule outlines the submission dates, and failure to meet them will result in exclusion. Once entries are submitted, fees are due even if a swimmer does not attend.

Event Selection

Selections are based on long-term swimmer development. Swimmers may switch strokes to improve skills and motivation. Team challenges and events like "200 fly" might be assigned for team points and personal growth. Time standards and coach evaluations determine meet participation.

General Meet Procedures

Arrive on time for warm-up and follow the coach's guidance. Stay with the team to conserve energy and foster team spirit. Parents are not allowed on the pool deck but can assist swimmers in returning to the coach when needed.

Marshalling

Check the marshalling board for event updates. Discuss strategy with the coach before marshalling and review performance after each event. Get your time from timers and, if time permits, visit parents.

Meet Preparation

Follow the training cycle to optimize performance. Missing phases (endurance, speed, etc.) can impact results. Conserve energy during long meets, including taking naps between sessions.

Team Attire

Wearing team uniforms is mandatory. Swimmers should label all personal equipment, including goggles.

Warmth

Keep warm with extra towels and a tracksuit. Avoid shivering, which wastes energy. Store belongings securely in a gym bag on the pool deck or in a locker.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about competitions and team procedures

What should my swimmer bring to meets?

Your swimmer should bring multiple towels, a water bottle, healthy snacks, WEST team cap and suit, extra goggles, warm clothes (track suit), and flip flops. Label all items with your swimmer's name.

How are events selected for swimmers?

Coaches select events based on each swimmer's development needs, technical abilities, and progression plan. This ensures balanced development across all strokes and distances appropriate for their age and skill level.

When should we arrive for meets?

Swimmers should arrive at least 15 minutes before the scheduled warm-up time. This allows time to check in with coaches, change, and prepare mentally for the session.

How can parents help during meets?

Parents can volunteer as timers or officials, help with team hospitality, and provide emotional support from the spectator area. Remember that coaches handle all technical aspects during the meet.
