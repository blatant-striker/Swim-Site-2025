Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to commonly asked questions about our swim programs and facilities.

Registration for our swim programs can be completed online through our registration portal, in person at our main office, or by phone. Please have your swimmer's information ready, including any previous swimming experience. For new swimmers, we recommend a skill assessment to determine the most appropriate program.

Required equipment varies by program level. Basic requirements include a proper-fitting swimsuit and goggles. Higher-level programs may require fins, kickboards, pull buoys, and paddles. Team swim caps are provided to all registered swimmers. A detailed equipment list will be provided upon registration.

Yes, we offer a one-time trial class for potential new swimmers. This allows both the swimmer and parents to experience our training environment and coaching style before committing to a full program. Please contact our office to schedule a trial session.

We understand that conflicts occasionally arise. Swimmers who miss a class due to illness or other circumstances may be eligible for a makeup session, subject to availability and proper notification. Please inform us at least 24 hours in advance of any absence when possible.

Parents are welcome to observe practices from our designated viewing areas. For younger swimmers (ages 12 and under), we recommend parents remain on-site during practices. For older swimmers, parents may drop off and pick up. We host periodic "Parent Observation Days" when parents can get a closer look at training sessions.
