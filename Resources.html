Officials Information

Resources for current and aspiring swim meet officials

Becoming an Official

Swim meets can't run without officials! Becoming an official is a great way to get involved in your child's sport and give back to the swimming community.

No experience is necessary to become an official. Training clinics are offered throughout the year, and you can learn as you go by shadowing experienced officials.

Benefits of Becoming an Official:
  • Best seat in the house to watch your swimmer
  • Free food and refreshments during meets
  • Team volunteer credit
  • Opportunity to attend higher-level meets
  • Be part of a fun and supportive community

General Information

Important information for swimmers and parents

Practice Policies
  • Arrive 10-15 minutes before practice
  • Bring water bottle, cap, goggles, and towel
  • Follow coach instructions at all times
  • Notify coaches of absences in advance
Meet Preparation
  • Pack multiple towels and extra goggles
  • Bring healthy snacks and water
  • Arrive 15 minutes before warm-up
  • Check in with coaches upon arrival
Team Equipment
  • Team cap required at meets
  • Team suit recommended for competitions
  • Equipment varies by training group
  • Team gear available through team store
Pool Schedules & Cancellations
  • Pool Cancellations 2024-2025
  • Check regularly for schedule updates
  • Emergency cancellations will be communicated via email
